The plan runs in three arcs: Month 1 builds PyTorch fluency plus real GPU understanding (CUDA/Triton kernels, profiling — this will feel familiar given how you approached SRX SPU/NPU internals), Month 2 has you rebuild GPT from a blank file and actually pretrain a 124M model on the Spark, then modernize it with RoPE, RMSNorm, SwiGLU and a KV cache, and Month 3 covers LoRA/QLoRA fine-tuning, quantization, and benchmarking llama.cpp vs vLLM vs TensorRT-LLM as local inference stacks.
The capstone is the part I'd most encourage: a RAG plus fine-tuned assistant built over your own SRX and 5G security notes, served locally from the Spark and queryable from your Mac. It exercises everything from the quarter, it's the workload the Spark's 128GB is genuinely built for, and you'd end up with something actually useful at work rather than another toy Shakespeare model.
Two practical tips before you start: do the "Weekend 0" setup first — the ARM64 wheel situation will bite you if you pip-install blindly instead of using NGC containers — and check NVIDIA's DGX Spark playbooks site early, since they've published guided recipes for several of the exact tasks in this plan.
